04 — Voice & Tone

How We Sound

Brightcove speaks with authority, clarity, and warmth. We are an enterprise partner — not a vendor. Lead with outcomes. Back them with proof. Never hype what you can demonstrate.

CONFIDENT, NOT ARROGANT
We have the receipts.
State facts, not boasts. "99% customer satisfaction" is more powerful than "best-in-class." Let outcomes do the talking.
PROFESSIONAL, NOT STIFF
Human, but buttoned-up.
Warm and direct — never casual or slang-heavy. No exclamation marks in headlines. Write for a senior decision-maker who values their time.
CLEAR, NOT SIMPLISTIC
Translate, don't dumb down.
Tech becomes outcomes. "CDN" becomes "global delivery network." Match vocabulary to audience, but never talk down to them.
PARTNER, NOT VENDOR
We're in this together.
Say "we help you" — not "our product does." The relationship is collaborative. Position Brightcove as an extension of the customer's team.

Tone by Context

Context Tone Example
Homepage / Hero Bold, declarative, outcome-first "Drive engagement through video."
Product Pages Clear, benefit-first, feature-supported "Stream to any device, without compromise."
Customer Stories Warm, specific, outcome-driven "How Wendy's trained employees 40% faster."
Email / Outreach Direct, concise, one clear CTA "Quick question about your video strategy."
Blog / Thought Leadership Authoritative, insightful, educational "Why AI is changing video publishing."
Technical Docs Precise, step-by-step, jargon-permitted "Configure your CDN endpoint as follows."

07 — Writing Rules

Do's and Don'ts

These rules apply to all Brightcove content — web, email, social, presentations, and proposals.

✓ Do

Lead every piece of content with a business outcome — not a feature.

✕ Don't

Open with product capabilities before the audience understands the "why."

✓ Do

Use active voice. "Brightcove powers video for 5,000+ brands."

✕ Don't

Write in passive voice. "Video is powered by Brightcove."

✓ Do

Back every claim with a number, customer name, or specific outcome.

✕ Don't

Use unsupported superlatives: "best-in-class," "world-class," "cutting-edge."

✓ Do

Use one primary CTA per section — clear, action-first, four words or fewer.

✕ Don't

Stack multiple CTAs in one section or use vague links like "Click Here."

✓ Do

Write headlines as bold, declarative statements. "Drive engagement through video."

✕ Don't

Add exclamation marks to headlines or professional copy.

✓ Do

Spell out numbers under 10 in prose. Use numerals for 10 and above.

✕ Don't

Round up statistics or invent metrics without a clear, attributable source.
