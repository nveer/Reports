Brightcove vs. Adobe Edge Delivery

Competitive overview — retention guide for customer conversations

⚠️ Key Insight

Adobe Edge Delivery Services (EDS) is a CMS and web performance tool — not a video platform. It has no native video hosting, streaming, or delivery. Customers moving to EDS would still need Brightcove to serve video. These are complementary tools, not replacements.

💬 Retention Talking Points

1
EDS is a CMS — not a video platform replacement

Any video on an EDS site must still be hosted externally. The customer would need Brightcove (or a competitor) regardless of adopting EDS for their web experience.

2
Understand what's really driving the conversation

Is it cost pressure? A push to consolidate Adobe licenses? A DAM frustration? Identifying the root cause usually reveals a misalignment of tools, not a true platform replacement need.

3
Anchor on video-specific use cases they care about

Live events, viewer analytics, monetization, OTT apps — map these back to the customer's actual needs. Whatever their video strategy is, EDS cannot fulfill it.

4
Position this as "and" not "or"

Brightcove + Adobe EDS can coexist. EDS handles the web experience; Brightcove powers the video. Frame the conversation around complementary tools, not a choice.

🏅

Proof point: Adobe Learning Manager runs on Brightcove's streaming infrastructure — a direct signal that Adobe's own products rely on Brightcove for video delivery, not their own platform.

🔍 Recommended Discovery Question

Ask the customer
"What specific gaps are you trying to solve with this move?"

This surfaces whether the customer is conflating two separate needs — web CMS vs. video platform — opening the door to a "you can have both" conversation rather than a replacement scenario.
