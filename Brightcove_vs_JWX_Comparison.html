Brightcove vs JWX

A capability comparison to support Jukin's video platform evaluation — covering ad technology, platform integration, and content experience.

Ad Technology
Capability JWX (JW Player + Connatix) Brightcove
Ad Server Proprietary Ecosystem
Ad delivery is routed through the JWX/Connatix ad server. Publishers are tied to their demand marketplace, limiting flexibility to choose or switch ad technology partners. 		Fully Agnostic
Works with any VAST-compliant ad server — GAM, FreeWheel, SpringServe, and more. Jukin owns and controls the ad strategy without dependency on Brightcove's monetization network.
SSAI
Server-Side Ad Insertion		 Yes — Ecosystem Only
SSAI for live and VOD, but delivery is locked to JWX's ad server. Publishers cannot pair SSAI with their own preferred ad server or demand partners. 		Yes — Fully Agnostic
Full SSAI for live and VOD — compatible with any ad server: GAM, FreeWheel, DFP, SpotX, SpringServe. Jukin retains full control of demand relationships even in SSAI mode.
CSAI + SSAI
Ad-Block Fallback		 Not Available
JWX requires choosing one ad insertion method. There is no automatic fallback between CSAI and SSAI, meaning ad-blocked impressions are simply lost. 		Brightcove Exclusive
Default CSAI → ad blocker detected → automatic SSAI fallback. Ads are server-stitched and invisible to blockers, recovering 20–30% of ad revenue that would otherwise be lost.
Since Jukin has no SSAI today, this is net-new incremental revenue from day one — without changing the viewer experience.
Programmatic Demand Yes — Revenue Shared
Access to Connatix Exchange (Trade Desk via OpenPath, Prebid.js). Connatix fills remnant inventory and retains 30–50% of that revenue. Demand relationships are owned by Connatix, not the publisher. 		Yes — Publisher Keeps 100%
Full programmatic access via publisher's own ad server and Prebid header bidding. Jukin owns every demand relationship directly — no revenue share, no intermediary.
Industry standard: programmatic runs via CSAI; direct-sold inventory can leverage SSAI. This is not a Brightcove limitation — it applies to all platforms.
Outstream / Floating Player Yes — Revenue Shared
Dedicated Connatix sticky/floating outstream unit. Connatix sources and serves the ads, retaining 30–50% of revenue generated. Publishers receive the remainder with no direct control over demand. 		Yes — Publisher Keeps 100%
Brightcove Outstream Player with Pinning mode (Picture-in-Picture plugin) — identical sticky/floating experience. Fully integrated with Jukin's own ad server. No revenue sharing — every ad dollar goes to Jukin.
Remnant Fill /
Rev-Share Model		 Built-In
Connatix fills unsold remnant inventory automatically through its demand marketplace and shares the revenue with the publisher. For publishers who lack their own programmatic sales team, this is incremental revenue they wouldn't otherwise capture. 		Not Available
Brightcove does not operate a demand marketplace or fill remnant inventory on the publisher's behalf. Jukin would need to manage their own programmatic demand relationships (header bidding, SSPs, etc.) or leave that inventory unfilled.
Trade-off: Full revenue ownership means full responsibility. If Jukin has the ad ops capability, they keep 100%. If not, the Connatix model provides a safety net — at the cost of 30–50% of that revenue.
Platform & Integration
Capability JWX (JW Player + Connatix) Brightcove
WordPress Integration Plugin Available
JWX offers a WordPress plugin, but deeper configurations typically require custom development embedded directly in WordPress rather than the platform itself. 		Native Plugin
Brightcove Video Connect — free, open-source plugin natively maintained by Brightcove. Video upload, playlists, shortcodes, native block editor support, and multi-account management. Out-of-the-box with no custom engineering required.
Migration Path N/A
No migration, but in-stream ad stack rebuild and WordPress decoupling work remain regardless. 		Low Complexity
Jukin's 300K+ video library already lives in Brightcove. JWX hosts ~6–7K videos. Consolidating onto Brightcove means migrating a fraction of content — the heavy lifting is done.
Platform Stability Competing Products Merged
JWX formed October 2024 by merging JW Player and Connatix — two platforms with overlapping capabilities. New CEO installed March 2025, product roadmap still consolidating. When competing features merge, something gets deprioritized. 		Single Focused Platform
Brightcove is a single, purpose-built video platform — not two merged products rationalizing overlap. Continued investment under Bending Spoons ownership with a clear, unified product direction. Jukin has been a customer since 2018 with a dedicated CSM.

Summary

Where Brightcove Leads
  • CSAI + SSAI ad-block fallback — recovers 20–30% of lost ad revenue
  • Fully ad-server agnostic across SSAI and programmatic
  • No revenue sharing — Jukin keeps 100% of ad revenue generated
  • Vertical video GA since January 2025 — one year ahead of JWX
  • Native WordPress plugin — no custom engineering required
  • Native interactive video via HapYak (acquired 2021)
  • Established platform with no mid-merger disruption
  • 300K+ videos already in Brightcove — migration nearly complete
Where JWX Competes
  • Connatix fills remnant inventory (revenue shared with publisher)
  • Integrated player + ad marketplace for publishers prioritizing ease
  • No migration effort for existing JWX customers
  • Vertical video product now available (launched Feb 2026)
  • Contextual article-matching for content recommendations
The bottom line: JWX bundles the player with its ad network — publishers access demand through Connatix and share the revenue. Brightcove provides the platform and lets Jukin bring their own ad stack, keep 100% of what they earn, and recover ad-blocked impressions through a CSAI + SSAI fallback that JWX simply does not offer. More control, more revenue, on a stable platform Jukin already knows.
